  • Non-analytic attributes

Business Value · Data Translation

KEY INSIGHTS

01 · Conversion Rate
35.45% — 2× Industry Avg
  • Stable across all 20 seasons — not random variance
  • High-volume years = same efficiency, more shots
  • Shot selection is disciplined — quality over quantity
02 · Power vs Accuracy Tradeoff
Placement Wins
  • Best conversion: 9–40 unit distance range
  • High power + long range = low conversion density
  • "Higher power correlates with lower accuracy"
  • Precision finisher, not a long-range specialist
03 · Late-Game Composure
46–75 min Peak
  • Peak output when defensive lines drop
  • 90+ goals = elite endurance outlier
  • Knockout: more evenly distributed — tactical discipline
  • Data supports: deploy as a late-game impact player
04 · Pressure Performance
No Home/Away Drop
  • Shot distribution identical H vs A
  • Conversion holds in hostile away environments
  • Knockout efficiency — fewer shots, maintained rate
  • Psychological resilience visible in the data

Why 29K clean rows?   153,485 RAW → minus 7,815 null shot_id_number rows = 145,670 key-valid. Each unique (match_id, shot_id_number) appears exactly 5× in RAW (avg & max = 5) — a systematic ingestion repeat across batches. QUALIFY ROW_NUMBER() = 1 deduplicates: 145,670 ÷ 5 = 29,134 clean rows. No business data lost — pure structural deduplication.

Senior Engineering Thinking

LIMITATIONS & CONFIDENCE

Dataset Limitations

  • Single player — no peer benchmarking
  • No xG (expected goals) metric available
  • No defensive/tactical context
  • Free kick vs open play not distinguished
  • No opponent-level data
  • Pre-1996 career not covered

Data Quality Caveats

  • ~18% conflict rate on 3 key fields
  • Distance/power excluded from precision KPIs
  • Knockout classification ambiguity remains

Confidence Assessment

  • ✔ High confidence — structural correctness
  • ✔ High confidence — volume-based metrics
  • △ Moderate confidence — distance precision
  • △ Moderate confidence — contextual completeness
